About the game

Description

Essence of the Tjikko is a retro 3D puzzle-adventure set inside a 7-floor underground botanical laboratory - a vertical dungeon of 30+ interconnected rooms where simple actions combine into surprising solutions.

The gameplay is built on basic mechanics: climbing ledges, pushing crates, carrying energy bulbs, swimming. But they are layered with 4 seasonal fruits that change your abilities and your appearance - unlocking emergent puzzle-solving that rewards curiosity (or brute force):

Spring
Water bouncing
Summer
Blazing speed
Autumn
Ethereal vision
Winter
Frosted jump

The world treats plant networks like operating systems - roots as cables, sap as data, botanical mechanisms responding to inputs like living computers. Nature and technology share the same language here, and the hero, half-plant, half-child, is fluent in both.

Visually, the game is a sincere handcraft, made with love and care. The choice of a 4:3 aspect ratio isn't nostalgia for its own sake - it's a deliberate artistic decision. Those proportions feel closer to a fairy tale than a simulation. The world fits inside the frame like an illustration in a storybook.

Inspired by the dungeons of the greatest Zelda titles, but telling its own story - an allegory of childhood, growth, and the instinct to reach the light.

Development story

History

Essence of the Tjikko was born in Annecy - a picturesque city where nature performs in a grand theatre. A pristine lake as its stage, mountains as a canvas, framing each season like a different act. Working at Ubisoft in 2018, I started writing the world of Erdeven in my spare time, trying to bridge Annecy's topology with biome archetypes and environmental symbolism - something I'd been thinking about since reading The Uses of Enchantment during my geology studies.

When the world grew uncertain in 2021, it felt like the right moment to build something meaningful as an artist. I launched as a freelance, and gave myself 9 months to ship the first version of Tjikko. It came out in February 2022 - I brought it to small conventions, introduced it to streamers... The result was honest but incomplete. Like a game you put back on the shelf when you're not quite ready for it yet - knowing you'll come back when you are.

The years that followed were formative ones in the industry, to say the least. And when the stars finally aligned in September 2025, I returned to Tjikko with a clear objective: the Remastered Edition for Zelda's 40th anniversary on February 21st, 2026.

The remaster reflects everything those years taught me. At least half the game was remade - technically and artistically. The lighting, the textures, the level design details, the difficulty balance...

Looking back, Tjikko and I evolved through this journey, thanks to the support of the friends I met. Former colleagues lent their eyes and instincts to design and visuals. Native speakers across 10 languages trusted the story enough to carry it into their own tongue. And my friend Philippe Reinhart's music gave the world its emotional heartbeat.

It may be a long way to create a game, but it's totally worth it to see it growing. And I can't wait to see the next act.

What's next

Next Steps

Essence of the Tjikko - Prologue is the first chapter of a larger adventure. The full game envisions a vast underground world: five dungeons, a central hub carved beneath the roots of the great Tjikko, with subterranean lakes, temples, jungles and villages - a civilization frozen in time. The next chapter is currently in development.

The developer

Maxime Carcaillon
Solo Developer · Paris, France
m.carcaillon@gmail.com mylink.la/tjikkogame artstation.com/xamix

Trained as a geologist before turning to game art, Maxime brings an unusual eye to world-building - one shaped by topology, natural systems, and the symbolism of landscapes. After contributing to titles like Tom Clancy's The Division 2 and AGOS at Ubisoft, he founded La Revria Studio to develop the games he always wanted to see exist.

La Revria Studio is a one-person operation with a network of collaborators - composers, translators, playtesters - who believe in the project. The goal: handcrafted adventure games with artistic ambition and a retro soul.
